Hi Hiran,

If you decide to work on the OpenRecord Gantt Chart plugin next, you 
should look into using the Timeline widget from the SIMILE project. 
Adam Souzis just posted a message about it to the openrecord-dev list 
yesterday -- I don't know if you've had a chance to look at it yet.

Here are a few demo pages...

Try dragging left and right, and clicking on the dots and bars:
  http://simile.mit.edu/timeline/

Check out the text-highlighting feature, by typing in one of the colored 
text-boxes:
  http://simile.mit.edu/timeline/examples/religions/religions.html

And here's one that has 4 bands to scroll time at 4 different time-scales:
  http://simile.mit.edu/timeline/examples/dinosaurs/dinosaurs2.html

Also available in both vertical and horizontal:
  http://simile.mit.edu/timeline/docs/basics.html

And it seems be extremely well documented:
  http://simile.mit.edu/timeline/docs/

The code is available under a BSD license, so we can safely include that 
code in our own SVN repository as long as we're careful to preserve and 
include the original copyright and license statements.

The Timeline widget can be populated with data in JSON format, so it 
should be fairly easy to feed it data from OpenRecord.  Realistically, I 
imagine that there'll be a ton of little issues involved in actually 
getting the Timeline wired up to work within OpenRecord, but the work 
involved in resolving those issues is probably a small fraction of what 
it would take to do a good Gantt Chart from scratch.  There's also some 
overlap/duplication where both the Timeline widget and Dojo each have 
their own way of doing something (localization, for example), but that's 
okay.

:o) Brian
